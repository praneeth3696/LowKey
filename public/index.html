how audio LSB works
each 16-bit audio sample's least significant bit is replaced with one bit of your message. the amplitude changes by ±1 out of 65535 — completely inaudible. a 44100 Hz stereo 10s file = ~880KB = ~110KB capacity.

The Craft of Steganography
The art of hiding messages in plain sight — since ancient times
01
Image LSB
modify the least significant bit of each RGB pixel channel. change is ≤1/255 — invisible to the eye. a 1080×720 image holds ~97KB.
02
Audio LSB
replace LSB of 16-bit WAV samples. amplitude changes by ±1 out of 65535. inaudible. works on any uncompressed WAV.
03
Zero-Width Text
encode binary with U+200B/U+200C invisible Unicode characters injected between normal glyphs. invisible to readers, readable in hex.
04
Metadata Injection
hide data in PNG tEXt chunks, EXIF comment fields, or IPTC records. the image looks identical. data survives most viewers.
05
Chi-Square Analysis
statistical attack on LSB replacement. in natural images, pairs (2k,2k+1) differ. LSB encoding equalizes them — detectable.
06
LSB Plane View
amplify every pixel's LSB to 0 or 255. natural images show random noise. stego images reveal ordered structure.
historical context

steganography predates digital computers. ancient greeks tattooed secrets on shaved scalps. WWII used invisible ink, microdots hidden in newspapers, and null ciphers in ordinary letters. today: digital watermarking protects copyright. journalists use stego for source protection. nation-state actors embed C2 channels in image comment fields. the goal has never changed — the best hidden message is one whose existence goes unsuspected.
